NARGIS BEAUTY

A Rebirth in Materiality.

Nargis Beauty is a premium botanical skincare brand rooted in the philosophy that luxury is a tactile narrative. The objective was to craft a high-conversion brand identity that bridges the gap between ancient apothecary heritage and severe, modern aesthetic precision.

Strategic Positioning

Material
Storytelling.

We moved away from sterile, conventional beauty tropes, leaning heavily into the high-frequency vibrations of texture—from the grain of raw stone and detoxifying clays to the fluidity of silk and cold-pressed artisan oils.

The brand does not just harvest botanicals; it listens to the season’s whisper to find the earth's most honest expression. Intersecting elite branding with scalable physical and digital touchpoints, the resulting identity is an exercise in material storytelling—designed to be felt through the screen.

"If you can feel the texture through the screen, the brand is alive."

Visual Identity

The Architecture.

In a market saturated with severe, minimalist wordmarks, we made the deliberate, strategic choice to anchor Nargis Beauty with a heritage-driven, illustrative symbol. This grounds the brand in human touch and approachability.

Typography

Structural Tension

Ogg Roman (Elegance): The anchor of the visual soul. A lush, calligraphic, high-contrast serif that embodies the brand's heritage and premium aspirations.

Neue Montreal (Utility & Precision): The structural baseline. A clean, Swiss-inspired workhorse ensuring absolute clarity and modern functionality across web ecosystems and packaging formats.

Color System

Psychology of Palette

Moving away from stark contrasts, we utilize earthy depth:

Deep Plum (2A1A26): Authority and Luxury Depth.
Terracotta (C47A6B): The Earthy Heartbeat, used with mathematical restraint.
Cream (F7F5F0): Foundations & Raw Skin, providing essential breathing room.

Packaging

Apothecary Modernism

The packaging architecture was designed as a permanent heirloom. Weighted amber glass protects volatile botanical extractions—like our rare Narcissus Absolue—while raw copper closures and scalloped labeling evoke a sense of grounding permanence.
